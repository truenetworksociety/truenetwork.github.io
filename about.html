What the Society advances

TrueNetwork Community Solutions Society works at the intersection of community knowledge, participatory research, digital infrastructure, and collaborative innovation. We support nonprofit organizations, governments, municipalities, and community partners through evidence-informed planning, co-creation, participatory evaluation, and community development.

A
Applied and participatory We prioritize practice-based knowledge grounded in community realities and lived experience.
D
Digitally enabled We build tools and shared systems that make knowledge visible, usable, and actionable.

Kishore Seetharaman

Kishore Seetharaman is a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Gerontology, at Simon Fraser University, Canada. His interdisciplinary background integrates perspectives from the fields of gerontology, environmental psychology, and architectural design. His areas of research include aging, dementia, disability, built environment, and design. His current research explores urban accessibility and dementia-inclusivity through an inclusive-universal design approach. His research employs qualitative research methods to explore the role of the outdoor environment in shaping the out-of-home activity of people aging with different types of disabilities.

Faryar Mohammadi

I have never been able to walk into a community without wondering what is possible here. Whether I am meeting a senior, a child, a caregiver, an artist, a volunteer, a researcher, a professional, a business owner, or a neighbour, I naturally look for the gifts, relationships, and opportunities that already exist beneath the surface. Some mentors and colleagues have described me as an “Infinite Social Possibility Producer.” While I would not have chosen that title for myself, I have come to appreciate it because it reflects how I see people and communities—not as collections of needs and problems, but as sources of creativity, wisdom, connection, and possibility. My background is in Psychology, Recreation Management, and Asset-Based Community Development (ABCD). In 2024, I co-founded the Dementia Co-Creation Academy, where I have had the privilege of learning alongside people living with dementia, caregivers, volunteers, artists, students, and community members. Today, I also support the Board and leadership team of True Network Community Solutions. Over the years, I have found it impossible to separate therapy, education, recreation, and community development. People learn while they play, heal while they connect, find joy in creating with others, teach through their stories, and grow when they are invited to contribute. I believe every person carries gifts that can strengthen a community, and that our vulnerabilities, when met with dignity, understanding, and belonging, can become sources of connection and contribution. My experience has taught me that every community contains untapped strengths, stories, relationships, and possibilities waiting to emerge. Funding, research, technology, and planning all matter, but lasting change happens when people recognize their own capacity—and the capacity of those around them—to create, care, learn, teach, and shape the future together. Community building is not simply the work I do; it is how I understand the world and choose to live each day.
